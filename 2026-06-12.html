The Headlines
  • Sefton Dockstader leads the league at .933, with Claude Timpson (.905) right behind — the only two .900 bats in the league.
  • The Lefty Looseys lead everything: best team average (.626) and the best draft grade (+7.49 total z). Ten of their twelve players are hitting .500 or better.
  • The draft already predicts performance: pick round correlates with average at r = −0.51, from .680 (round 1) down to .338 (round 12).
  • The steal of the draft so far is Sean Hammon — a 12th-round pick hitting .769, tenth-best in the entire league.
  • Discipline is nearly universal: 115 of 144 players have yet to cause an out (33 caused outs league-wide, total).

One thing that jumps out about every team

  • Lefty Looseys — Leading the league in average and depth (ten of twelve at .500+), with the league's best bat (Sefton, .933). Also the widest player spread (σ = .227) — stars and strugglers on the same bench.
  • Fellowship of the Swing — The quiet contender: second in average, ten players in the .500 Club, and just 2 caused outs.
  • Diamonds and Dirtbags — One caused out all season, and third place without a single .800 bat. Their best hitter is at "only" .750 — this is a depth play.
  • Good Guys — Second-best raw hitting (.619), but the league-worst 6 caused outs already cost them .041 of average, the biggest discipline penalty in the league.
  • Pliggas — Top-heavy: Claude Timpson is at .905 and nobody logs more team at-bats (165). The gap between their stars and their bench (σ = .203) is real.
  • Youre Saying Theres A Chance — Nine of twelve at .500+ and quietly tidy (3 caused outs). Solidly mid-table with upside.
  • Pure Breads — The second-widest spread in the league (σ = .218): five bats at .667 or better, and a bottom four hitting .333 or worse.
  • Danites — Found a top-15 league bat in round 6 (Noah Dockstader, .750), but their first-rounder Heber Williams (.500) sits bottom-three in round 1.
  • Stars and Strikes — Clean (1 caused out) but thin: only six players at .500+, and their best bat (Sam Guy, .714) ranks just 24th league-wide.
  • Ellites — Tenth place, but Bryan Dockstader (.727) is a legitimate third-round hit. The problem is everything after round 5.
  • Playas — The only team with zero caused outs — and the fewest at-bats in the league (100). Perfect discipline, missing bodies.
  • Slamma Jammas — Dead last by 58 points of average. Half the roster is in the .500 Club and it doesn't matter: three of their bats are at .182 or worse.
