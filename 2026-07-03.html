The Headlines
  • The draft is scary-accurate: round drafted predicts average with r = −0.61, decaying almost perfectly from .726 (round 1) to .384 (round 12).
  • Round 6 is the market inefficiency — sixth-rounders out-hit rounds 4, 5, and nearly 3. Three of the league's best sleepers came from it.
  • The Lefty Looseys (Sefton's team) won the draft (+5.6 total z, best in the league) and it shows: 11 of their 12 players hit .500+.
  • The Good Guys (Gideon's team) out-hit everyone by raw average (.639) — and caused outs (15, most in the league) knock them all the way to 4th.
  • The single biggest steal, by expectation, was a 12th-round pick: Sean Hammon (#140 of 144), .609, a full +2.06 z over his round.
  • New this edition: Cuervo Timpson hit .957 and seized the league lead (What Changed) — and The Verdict now grades all 144 picks: 82 justified theirs, 22 were priced exactly right, and the Dream Team clears an average roster by +53.8 net hits.
