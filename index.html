All averages are the site's adjusted average, reverse-engineered from the data: (hits − caused outs) ÷ at-bats, verified exact on all 144 rows. The Headlines
  • The draft is scary-accurate: round drafted predicts average with r = −0.61, decaying almost perfectly from .726 (round 1) to .384 (round 12).
  • Round 6 is the market inefficiency — sixth-rounders out-hit rounds 4, 5, and nearly 3. Three of the league's best sleepers came from it.
  • The Lefty Looseys won the draft (+5.6 total z, best in the league) and it shows: 11 of their 12 players hit .500+.
  • The Good Guys out-hit everyone by raw average (.639) — and caused outs (15, most in the league) knock them all the way to 4th.
  • The single biggest steal, by expectation, was a 12th-round pick: Sean Hammon, .609, a full +2.06 z over his round.
  • New this edition: Cuervo Timpson hit .957 over the last three weeks and seized the league lead — the full June 12 → July 3 movers report is in Section 8.

One thing that jumps out about every team

  • Diamonds and Dirtbags — Best team average in the league, and the most boom-or-bust roster (highest player spread, σ = .199). Ten hitters above .500 papers over the busts.
  • Lefty Looseys — The deepest lineup in the league: 11 of 12 players above .500. Nobody else has more than ten. Also collected three of the sleeper file's names (Seth Timpson, Riley Barlow, Jayla Dockstader).
  • Youre Saying Theres A Chance — 3 caused outs all season. Next-best team has 5; the Good Guys have 15. Pure plate discipline is worth about a podium spot.
  • Good Guys — The league's great tragedy: best raw hitting in the league (.639), worst caused-out total (15). The penalty drops them from 1st to 4th. Benjiman Barlow alone caused 4.
  • Fellowship of the Swing — Steady and anonymous: solid 5th with the lowest team ceiling among contenders (best bat "only" .708). Death by a dozen good-not-great hitters.
  • Pure Breads — Found Anson Johnson (R9, .682) and Ryan Hammon (R5, .714) late, which is the only reason a middling draft class landed 6th.
  • Pliggas — Iron-horse team: league-most 314 at-bats, and they walked away with the draft's biggest steal (Sean Hammon, R12).
  • Stars and Strikes — Own the MVP (Cuervo, .842) and only six .500 hitters. The most top-heavy contender in the league.
  • Slamma Jammas — A team of extremes: the round-11 steal of the draft (Maureen Williams) and the league's worst bust (Layla Hammon, .091, R10) on the same bench.
  • Danites — Landed a top-10 league bat in round 6 (Noah Dockstader) and it still couldn't offset the only failed first-round pick in the league (Heber Williams, .577).
  • Ellites — Lowest ceiling in the league: no hitter above .684. Eight players cleared .500 and it still wasn't enough — no stars, no chance.
  • Playas — Their real opponent is attendance: 186 team at-bats vs. the Pliggas' 314. Ironically the most consistent roster in the league (σ = .120) — consistently short-handed.
